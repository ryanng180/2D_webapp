What is the Pandemic Food security predictor?

The Pandemic Food security predictor predicts the Prevalence of Food Insecurity within the country in the time of a pandemic. It is most accurate when based on the Human Development Index and Total Population of the country.

This allows the countries to prepare for the extent of impact that the pandemic has on their food security issue, allowing them to act swiftly and accurately.

Problem Statement Introduction

How might we predict the prevalence of food insecurity within a country amid a pandemic, given the country’s social, political, and economic factors?

We aim to categorize this prediction either as Moderate or Severe. This classification allows us to assess the anticipated level of food insecurity within a nation by considering its social, political, and economic landscape amidst a pandemic, offering insight into the likely severity of food insecurity based on their specific socio-political-economic conditions.

Limitations of Model

From the analysis process, we were also able to conclude that not all factors will have an impact on the food security of a country. Many factors that we’ve initially considered turned out to be ineffective in modelling the correlation between the predictor and response variables due to the lack of correlation between them.

As such, we’ve narrowed down to just two predictor variables in our multi-regression line, which are HDI and Total population of a country.

However, even after identifying the appropriate predictor variables, we have to also note that there are other considerations involved when using the model in decision-making. For example, we have to note that the Human Development Index is based on multiple dimensions and countries with high HDI will likely always be food secure.

A possible improvement for future models is that we only take into account countries below a specific range of HDI as these are the countries that are more prone to food insecurity in times of a pandemic.

Conclusion

As such, while our model may be limited in its ability to give the exact number of people experiencing food insecurity in a country during a pandemic, it is still able to give an accurate prediction on the extent of impact that the pandemic will have on their food security based on their HDI and Total population in the country.
